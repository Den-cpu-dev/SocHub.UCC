Master's Programs

Advanced degrees in Sociology and Anthropology

Master of Philosophy (MPhil) in Sociology

A two-year research-focused program designed to develop advanced expertise in sociological theory, research methods, and contemporary social issues. Students undertake intensive coursework and independent research projects, culminating in a thesis.

Year 1 - First Semester

  • SOC 501 Advanced Sociological Theory CORE
  • SOC 502 Research Design and Methodology CORE

Year 1 - Second Semester

  • SOC 503 Qualitative Research Methods CORE
  • SOC 504 Quantitative Analysis & Statistics CORE
  • SOC 505 African Sociology and Development (Elective)

Year 2 - First Semester

  • SOC 601 Seminar in Specialized Topics CORE
  • SOC 602 Research Proposal Development CORE

Year 2 - Second Semester

  • SOC 699 MPhil Thesis CORE

Master of Philosophy (MPhil) in Anthropology

A specialized two-year program combining coursework and research in cultural, physical, and applied anthropology. Prepares students for doctoral work or professional practice in anthropological fields.

Year 1 - First Semester

  • ANT 501 Advanced Anthropological Theory CORE
  • ANT 502 Ethnographic Methods CORE

Year 1 - Second Semester

  • ANT 503 Cultural Analysis CORE
  • ANT 504 Applied Anthropology CORE
  • ANT 505 African Diaspora Studies (Elective)

Year 2 - First Semester

  • ANT 601 Seminar: Heritage and Development CORE
  • ANT 602 Thesis Proposal Seminar CORE

Year 2 - Second Semester

  • ANT 699 MPhil Thesis CORE

Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Sociology/Anthropology

A four-year doctoral program for advanced researchers. Students conduct original research in specialized areas, contribute to scholarly discourse, and develop expertise in their chosen fields. Graduates become independent researchers and academics.

Year 1 - Coursework & Seminars

  • SOC/ANT 701 Advanced Theory Seminar CORE
  • SOC/ANT 702 Research Methods Intensive CORE
  • SOC/ANT 703 Doctoral Seminar (Elective)

Year 2-3 - Research & Dissertation

  • SOC/ANT 800 Dissertation Research CORE
  • SOC/ANT 801 Literature Review & Research Design CORE

Year 4 - Dissertation Completion

  • SOC/ANT 899 PhD Dissertation CORE

Admission Requirements

For Master's Programs (MPhil)

  • Bachelor's degree in Sociology, Anthropology, or related social sciences
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 or equivalent
  • Statement of Purpose (500 words)
  • Two academic references
  • TOEFL/IELTS scores for international non-English speakers

For Doctoral Programs (PhD)

  • Master's degree in Sociology, Anthropology, or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.5 for graduate studies
  • Research proposal (1,000-1,500 words)
  • Writing sample (research paper or thesis chapter)
  • Three academic/professional references
  • Interview with prospective supervisor
  • TOEFL/IELTS for international applicants
